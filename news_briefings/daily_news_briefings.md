### Date:       21-Nov-2025

Federal Reserve:    "Top Fed official warns on risk hedge funds pose to $30tn Treasury market"

*Claire Jones in Washington and Kate Duguid in New York*

-   Lisa Cook, Fed Board Governor, voiced distain for hedge-fund's UST basis trades (pricing discrepencies) are impacting market functionality
-   In October 2025 the Fed published research highlighting a significant increase in offshore hedge funds' exposure to USTs
-   The basis trading strategy was at the centre of the March 2020 Pandemic Stress, 2019 Repo Crisis, etc
-    The Fed said last month it would halt the latest tightening programme starting 01-December, with speculation that the Fed could expand its T-bill holdings early next year
    -   This would effectively be a QE-style strategy acting independently amidst tightening


US Treasury Bonds:  "Foreign investors snap up Japanese government bonds as yields surge"

*Elettra Ardissino in London*
    
-   Foreign investors are heavy in long-dated JGBs, due to price drops (ie yield hikes)
-   Japanese data show foreigners long net $71.8bn (10y) (0ctober-2025); record high 3.41% JGBs (30y); 2.88% JGBs (20y)
-   Due to the effects of currency hedging, foreigners are recieving an added return differenial on long-dated JGBs = short-term_borrowing_costs_yen - other_majors
-   Recent regulations require JPN instutitonal investors to remain equally weighted in asset and liability positions, preventing large-scale bond participation.
-   BOJ are also reducing short-dated easing efforts, and hence tapering purchases of long-dated bonds (10s25s); essentially between insitiutions and the BOJ 10s25s are open season to foreigners; supply is high, prices are dropping, long-dated yields are being pushed up
